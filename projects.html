CATCH ME IF YOU CAN (2016)

Seeking fame and fortune, curious teenager Frank Abagnale. Jr. runs away from home to begin an unforgettable adventure. With nothing more than his boyish charm, a big imagination and millions of dollars in forged checks, Frank successfully poses as a pilot, a doctor and a lawyer. He's living the high life and is about to win the girl of his dreams. But when Frank's lies catch the attention of FBI agent Carl Hanratty, Carl pursues Frank across the country to make him pay for his crimes. Based on the hit film and the incredible true story, Catch Me If You Can, is the high-flying musical comedy about chasing your dreams and not getting caught. Nominated for 4 Tony awards, including Best Musical, Catch Me If You Can is with a book by Terrence McNally and score Marc Shaiman and Scott Wittman (Hairspray).

BURLESQUE (2015)

The Burlesque Lounge has its best days behind it. Tess (played by Cher), a retired dancer and owner of the venue, struggles to keep the aging theater alive, facing all kinds of financial and artistic challenges. With the Lounge's troupe members becoming increasingly distracted by personal problems and a threat coming from a wealthy businessman's quest to buy the spot from Tess, the good fortune seems to have abandoned the club altogether. Meanwhile, the life of Ali (played by Christina Aguilera, a small-town girl from Iowa, is about to change dramatically. Hired by Tess as a waitress at the Lounge, Ali escapes a hollow past and quickly falls in love with the art of burlesque. Backed by newfound friends amongst the theater's crew, she manages to fulfill her dreams of being on stage herself. Things take a dramatic turn though when Ali's big voice makes her become the main attraction of the revue.

ALL SHOOK UP (2014)

All Shook Up includes the famous songs of the King of Rock Elvis Presley, and is based on the comedy "Twelfth Night" by William Shakespeare. The musical takes place in a small town in the Central States, where loud music and public affection is prohibited by the conservative mayor. The town, however, is shaken when a young rocker stops there in search of a mechanic and manages to conquer the heart of local girl. What follows is a series of love misunderstandings that will make the public shed tears of laughter, along with the sound of hits like "Jailhouse Rock" and "Heartbreak Hotel".

MEMPHIS (2013)

From the underground clubs of the 1950s in Memphis, Tennessee comes the new and thrilling Broadway musical. “Memphis” captures the public’s hearts with its explosive dances, incredible songs and an exciting story of fame and forbidden love. With an original score, “Memphis“ focuses on a white radio host who wants to change the world and an up-and-coming black singer ready for her big break. In 2010, “Memphis” wins 4 Tony Awards including the category for best musical .The Broadway Performance Club at the American University in Bulgaria is the first amateur ensemble in the world to get the official rights for the recreation of the musical.

MOULIN ROUGE! (2011)

Moulin Rouge! is a romantic story about a young English writer, Christian, who falls in love with the terminally ill Satine - the star of Moulin Rouge, a cabaret dancer, and a courtesan. The year is 1900 and Christian has come to Paris in pursuit of the Bohemian revolution that has taken hold of the city's underworld. He meets a group of Bohemians who convince him to write a musical show called "Spectacular, Spectacular" to be performed at the cabaret Moulin Rouge where the rich and poor men alike come to be entertained by the dancers. There he meets the club's highest paid courtesan. Satine. The, love is made impossible after the owner of Moulin Rouge, Harold Zidler, offers Satine to the Duke of Monroth in return of a generous investment in "Spectacular, Spectacular". The Duke can fulfill Satine's dream and turn her into a famous real actress, but the price for that is her bociy. The director Baz Luhrmann brings this story to the big screen in the movie Moulin Rouge, starring Nicole Kidman and Ewan McGregor. The movie is based on the tragic story of Giuseppe Verdi's opera "La Traviata". It was nominated for eight Oscars and won two.

HAIRSPRAY (2010)

Hairspay tells the story of the chubby and talented teenager Tracy Turnblad. The year is 1962 and the wind of change blows all around Baltimore. Tracy, a girl with big hair and big dream, has one and only passion: to dance in the "Corny Collins Show". After meeting Seaweed she becomes convinced that everyone can make their dreams come true, regardless of appearance and skin color. The musical follows Tracy's transformation from a zealous fan of show of the 'nicest kids in town’, to a participant in the show as a real star. She is determined to show the world that everything it needs is a bit of rhythm and lots of HAIRSPRAY. Since its Broadway premiere in August 2002 until today, HAIRSPRAY wins the hearts of the audience and over 37 prestigious award, including for best musical. In 2007 the movie production of the musical is released, casting some of the most popular actors in Hollywood: John Travolta, Michelle Pfeiffer, Queen Latifah, Christopher Walken. Zac Efron and Nikki Blonsky. This colorful and crag comedy, with its witty humor and wild dance, manages always to make its audience cry of laughter, no matter whether in front of the big screen or in the theater.

WEST SIDE STORY (2009)

Inspired by the timeless story of Romeo and Juliet, West Side Story takes Shakespeare’s star-crossed lovers, and places them in the vibrant battleground of New York City’s West Side in the 1950s. In the midst of the deep-seated rivalry between the Puerto Rican gang, the Sharks, and the white gang, the Jets, Maria and Tony discover that ancient grudges are no match for true love. Their warring factions, however, refuse to back down, and the “rumbles”, romance, and resentment lead the bloody path to the lovers’ ultimate, tragic conclusion With soaring, sophisticated, and diverse melodies, energetic and athletic dance battles, and its remarkably salient social message, West Side Story remains one of American musical theatre’s most revolutionary and most loved treasures.

CHICAGO (2008)

Broadway’s longest-running American musical, Chicago, is a dazzling and satirical look at fame, justice, and the media machine. Set in 1920s Chicago and based on real-life murders and trials, Chicago follows Roxie Hart, a wannabe vaudevillian star who murders her lover and is arrested, despite her attempts to convince her pushover husband, Amos, to lie for her. In the Cook County Jail, Roxie meets her hero, the famed double-murderess and nightclub performer Velma Kelly. When both acquire the same lawyer, the greedy and lustful superstar, Billy Flynn, tensions come to a head as they vie for the spotlight-- though instead of onstage, they’re mugging for the flashbulb of the newspaper reporters. With catchy, sexy music and timeless lyrics by John Kander and Fred Ebb, and a funny, intelligent, and utterly engaging book by Kander and Bob Fosse, Chicago is a musical spectacular that is as addictive as gossip rags and as unforgettable as any trial of the century.
